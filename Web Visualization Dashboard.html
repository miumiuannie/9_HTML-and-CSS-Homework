    • Summary: Latitude vs.X

    This is an analysis and visualization of weather of 500+ cities across the world. The main objective was to showcase the relationship of various factors like temperature, humidity, cloudiness and wind speed with respect to the distance from the equator.
    For this analysis, we utilized a simple Python library, the OpenWeatherMap API, and a little common sense to create a representative model of weather across world cities. After randomly selecting at least 500 unique (non-repeat) cities based on latitude and longitude, a weather check was performed on each of the cities using a series of successive API. Then all different point of interests were plotted against latitude using Matplotlib.
    In conclusion, As expected, the weather becomes significantly warmer as one approaches the equator (0 Deg. Latitude). More interestingly, however, is the fact that the southern hemisphere tends to be warmer this time of year than the northern hemisphere.
    The site provides the source data and visualization created as part of the analysis, as well as explanations and descriptions of any treds.
